## Findings

The slice is still a real local target, but most of its inclusive time is a
child call:

- Body total: `8530471`
- Dynamic child edge: `8228252C -> 82287788`, body `5653971`
- Approximate exclusive local work: `2876500` (`33.72%`)
- Entries in the final interval: `170343`
- Ticks per entry: `6`

The hot local shape is a short matrix/vector-store span:

- PPC ops: `addi:7`, `vspltw:6`, `lvx128:4`, `stvewx:3`, `vmaddfp:3`,
  `lwz:4`, `lfs:2`, `cmpi:2`, one `bl`.
- HIR ops: `store_context:27`, `load_context:14`, `and:11`, `add:10`,
  `extract:9`, `load.1:6`, `splat:6`, `load_offset.1:4`, `context_barrier:3`.
- The three `stvewx` instructions are all masked-address plus dynamic
  `extract` plus `store.1` shapes at `82282580`, `82282584`, and `82282588`.
- The six `vspltw` operations are constant-index `extract` plus `splat`.
- The three `vmaddfp` operations lower through the heavy VMX FP path:
  VMX FPCR mode, scratch stack saves, `fmla`, NaN fixup, and denormal handling
  when required.
- The audit's conservative scalar/codegen floor is `112` A64-ish operations
  before counting the full heavy `mul_add_v128` body.

Existing speed logs do not provide a real A64 machine-code dump for this exact
function. The current audit therefore ties HIR to A64 lowering source paths:

- `load_context` / `store_context`:
  `src/xenia/cpu/backend/a64/a64_sequences.cc`
- guest `load.1` / `store.1`:
  `src/xenia/cpu/backend/a64/a64_seq_memory.cc`
- dynamic `extract i32`:
  `src/xenia/cpu/backend/a64/a64_seq_vector.cc`
- `mul_add v128`:
  `src/xenia/cpu/backend/a64/a64_sequences.cc`

## Decision

Do not start a broad `822824F0` vector peephole. The live body-backed target is
`8228252C-822825C4`, and even there the local part is only about `2.88M` ticks
after subtracting the `82287788` child edge.

The best next codegen experiment is a default-off, function/block-gated
`stvewx` lane-folding probe for this slice only, but only after proving the
stack-address lane assumptions. The promising shape is:

- `r9 = r1 + 0x50` followed by `stvewx vr13, r0, r9`
- `r8 = r1 + 0x50` and `r7 = 4` followed by `stvewx vr12, r8, r7`
- third store uses `r6 + 8` and needs a separate proof because `r6` is loaded
  from context

If the first two addresses are provably stack-aligned in this function, the
dynamic TBL-based extract path can become constant-lane extraction for those
two stores. Keep it gated to function `82282490` and guest PCs
`82282580/82282584`, with a rollback cvar. Do not include `82282588` until the
`r6` provenance is proven.
